Lean Six Sigma Project

Audit Calibration Improvement Initiative

Department: Quality Assurance

Prepared By: Kumar Raman (Role: Project Lead)

Duration: September 2024 - November 2024

Executive Summary

This Lean Six Sigma DMAIC project successfully addressed persistent audit calibration mismatches in Revenue Cycle Management AR workflows. Key achievements include a 6% improvement in calibration agreement (from 90% to 96%), a 65% reduction in monthly disputes (from 20 to 7), and a 60% improvement in feedback turnaround time (6.5 to 2.6 days), delivering an impressive annual ROI of 700%.

Define Phase

Outlined project goals focusing on reducing calibration variance between QA and Operations teams in AR workflows. Defined scope, key stakeholders, and project charter using DMAIC methodology.

Measure Phase

Collected data on current calibration mismatches, disputes, and time metrics. Established baseline metrics and measurement system analysis for improved data accuracy.

Analyze Phase

Identified root causes contributing to calibration mismatch including inconsistent audit guidelines adherence, feedback delays, and communication gaps.

Improve Phase

Implemented standardized audit calibration protocols, enhanced feedback communication channels, and trained staff leading to significant performance gains.

Control Phase

Established control charts and ongoing performance monitoring routines to sustain improvements and promptly address deviations.

Results and Conclusion

Metric Before Improvement After Improvement
Calibration Agreement 90% 96%
Monthly Disputes 20 7
Feedback Turnaround (days) 6.5 2.6

The project demonstrated clear success in reducing audit calibration variance, improving dispute resolution, and accelerating feedback times, resulting in elevated process performance and stakeholder satisfaction.
